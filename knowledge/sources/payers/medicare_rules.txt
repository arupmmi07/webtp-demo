MEDICARE PHYSICAL THERAPY COVERAGE
Guidelines for Rehabilitation Services
Based on CMS Regulations 2024
Reference: CMS Manual Publication 100-02, Chapter 15

================================================================================
SECTION 1: PLAN OF CARE (POC) REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

1.1 POC Authorization Mandate
-----------------------------
All Medicare patients MUST have an active Plan of Care (POC) before receiving
physical therapy services.

Requirements:
- POC must be established and signed by a physician or qualified non-physician
  practitioner (NPP)
- POC must specify: diagnosis, treatment goals, frequency, duration
- POC must be reviewed and re-certified every 90 days (or sooner if goals met)

1.2 Visit Limits
----------------
Medicare coverage allows:
- Initial Authorization: Up to 20 visits without requiring reauthorization
- After 20 visits: Requires medical necessity documentation and re-evaluation
- Maximum annual limit: Variable by condition (typically 100-150 visits/year)

1.3 POC Expiration
------------------
POC expires when:
- 90 days have passed since establishment/last certification
- Maximum visits specified in POC are reached
- Patient achieves treatment goals
- Treatment is deemed no longer medically necessary

Action Required:
- URGENT: If POC expires within 7 days
- WARNING: If POC expires within 14 days
- ROUTINE: If POC expires > 14 days

1.4 POC Approved Providers
--------------------------
For some Medicare Advantage plans:
- POC may specify approved provider list
- Provider changes may require physician approval
- System MUST verify provider is on approved list before assignment

================================================================================
SECTION 2: PROVIDER REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

2.1 Medicare Approval
---------------------
Provider must meet ALL of the following:
- Active state PT license
- NPI (National Provider Identifier) registered with Medicare
- Enrolled in Medicare as a qualified provider
- No exclusions or sanctions from federal healthcare programs

2.2 Billing Requirements
------------------------
- Provider must bill using correct CPT codes
- Documentation must support medical necessity
- Services must be provided by or under supervision of licensed PT

================================================================================
SECTION 3: COVERAGE RULES
================================================================================

3.1 Covered Services
--------------------
Medicare Part B covers:
- Therapeutic exercises
- Manual therapy
- Gait training
- Modalities (heat, cold, ultrasound, etc.)
- Evaluation and re-evaluation

3.2 Non-Covered Services
------------------------
Medicare does NOT cover:
- Maintenance therapy (unless under specific conditions)
- Services provided primarily for patient convenience
- Treatment without physician referral
- Services not deemed medically necessary

3.3 Medical Necessity
---------------------
Services must demonstrate:
- Reasonable expectation of improvement
- Skilled service requirement (cannot be self-administered)
- Complexity requiring PT expertise
- Documentation of functional limitations and goals

================================================================================
SECTION 4: SCHEDULING IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

4.1 Provider Verification
-------------------------
Before assigning Medicare patient to new provider, system MUST verify:
1. Provider is Medicare-approved (check NPI registration)
2. Provider is on POC approved list (if applicable)
3. POC is active and not expired
4. Remaining authorized visits > 0

4.2 POC Urgency Prioritization
-------------------------------
When scheduling replacements, prioritize patients by POC urgency:

HIGH PRIORITY:
- POC expires within 7 days
- Only 1-2 visits remaining on current authorization
- Patient at risk of no-show (losing covered visit)

MEDIUM PRIORITY:
- POC expires within 14 days
- 3-5 visits remaining

NORMAL PRIORITY:
- POC expires > 14 days
- Adequate visits remaining

4.3 Authorization Holds
-----------------------
If provider assignment fails multiple times for Medicare patient:
- Hold scheduling until POC reviewed
- Alert front desk manager (Sarah)
- May require physician contact for POC update/provider change approval

================================================================================
SECTION 5: COMPLIANCE NOTES
================================================================================

5.1 Audit Trail Requirements
-----------------------------
System must log:
- POC verification performed
- Provider Medicare enrollment verified
- Medical necessity determination basis
- All authorization checks

5.2 Denial Risk Mitigation
---------------------------
To reduce claim denials:
- Verify POC before every appointment
- Ensure provider credentials current
- Document medical necessity in each session note
- Track visits against authorized limits

5.3 Appeals Process
-------------------
If Medicare denies coverage:
- Review documentation for completeness
- Check for POC/provider mismatch
- File appeal with supporting clinical documentation
- Timeline: 120 days from denial date
