METRO PHYSICAL THERAPY
Scheduling & Provider Assignment Policy
Effective Date: January 2024
Version: 1.0

================================================================================
SECTION 1: PROVIDER MATCHING RULES
================================================================================

1.1 Skills & Certification Requirements
----------------------------------------
The system must ensure providers have appropriate qualifications for each
patient's condition:

- Orthopedic Cases:
  * Requires "Orthopedic PT" certification
  * Examples: post-surgical knee, hip replacement, joint rehabilitation
  
- Neurological Cases:
  * Requires "Neurological PT" certification
  * Examples: stroke recovery, Parkinson's, spinal cord injury
  
- Pediatric Cases:
  * Requires "Pediatric PT" certification
  * Age restriction: Patients under 18 years

- General Cases:
  * General PT acceptable for: back pain, muscle strain, general mobility
  * If no specialist available: General PT with 5+ years experience acceptable

1.2 Location Constraints
------------------------
Provider location must be convenient for patient access:

- Default Maximum Distance: 10 miles from patient's home address
- Measurement: Calculated as driving distance (not straight-line)
- Exception: Patient may explicitly request specific provider regardless of distance
- Urban areas: Consider public transit accessibility
- Rural areas: May extend to 25 miles if no closer options

1.3 Availability Requirements
------------------------------
Provider must have availability within patient's scheduling window:

- Default Window: Â±3 days from original appointment date
- Preferred: Same day and time as original appointment
- Patient preferences (if specified):
  * Preferred days: Monday-Friday
  * Preferred times: Morning (7-12), Afternoon (12-5), Evening (5-8)

================================================================================
SECTION 2: SPECIAL CASES
================================================================================

2.1 Gender Preference
---------------------
When patient expresses gender preference:
- MUST honor if "required" preference
- SHOULD honor if "preferred" (unless no options available)
- Document reason if unable to fulfill

2.2 Continuity of Care
----------------------
When patient has prior history with providers:
- Prioritize providers patient has seen before
- Weight: High priority (40 points in scoring)
- Especially important for chronic conditions

2.3 Insurance/Payer Compliance
-------------------------------
Provider must be authorized for patient's insurance:
- Refer to payer-specific rules (see Medicare_Coverage_Guidelines)
- Check POC (Plan of Care) authorization
- Verify provider NPI registration
